Group Reflection

Peter Kassis

What went well

We all work really well on our own. I feel the team are all responsible and take pride in their work. Everyone jumped on their work and got it done.

What could be improved

I feel we all could give each other more feedback or check each other's work more, or even allocate one person to check over everyone's work to ensure no tasks are missed. Communication, I feel that communication could be improved on, maybe we needed two meetings per week. As we started late, the time is short.
I think we should of uploaded our work as we did it to github. This gives everyone an idea where everyone is up to without the need to ask people.

Future Group work

For future group work, I will initiate a discussion about everyone's work, each person needs to tell us what their work involves, and we need to assess as a group if they have it under control or where they need to adjust. One at the start and one at the end, where they run over their work, and we can confirm it ticks the boxes.
I i will also recommend everyone uploads to github from start to finish.

At least one thing that was surprising

I was surprised my issue was the same this time as it was last time. The communication issue reappeared and feedback.

At least one thing that you have learned about groups

Communication is so crucial; we need to be on top of our work and each other's. As a team, we need to each have our work and our commitment to the team as a priority. It’s a little challenging to have a thorough understanding of the entire project, ensure everyone has a thorough understanding of their tasks and then progress.

GitHub reflects

My contributions are well documented in GitHub. As I adjusted along the way and used GitHub as my shared platform as well as Microsoft teams. I don’t think it shows the other team members history accurately, as everyone uploaded towards the end.If you're checking only GitHubs log, you will not get a good reflection of the other team members commitment.

William Truong

We may have started the assignment 3 project later than most people but as soon as we all knew what had to be done, we all started to do our individual tasks as quickly as possible. It's a little surprising that we may finish this assignment quicker than I had thought, ignoring the fact that it's due very soon. The communication between the team members was pretty good, using Microsoft Teams to post inquiries or suggestions was helpful for me for this project. Group meetings were done mostly weekly, and I think that weekly is good enough anymore will be a bit excessive as we all are busy people. Not everyone will show up to the meetings, but team members would later eventually watch the recordings to understand what has happened, but it would be better if everyone had a meeting in the present. One thing that I have learnt working as a group is that I'm not usually someone who works well with a group, but this group surprisingly wasn't so bad to work with as they had my back on stuff that I'm weak at.

Blade Sabre

We started assignment 3 fairly late. Missing out on the first two crucial weeks for the project. Everyone, including myself, was busy with weekly course tasks as well as work. Still, I feel that better communication could have gotten the team back together sooner and I wouldn’t have had to intervene as the new team leader. I don’t regret becoming the de facto leader as I have learned many things I wouldn’t have otherwise. But one thing that I did learn that surprised me was that I am not a good team leader. I am just not built for it. I feel that I have let the team down with bad leadership and poor direction. Someone like Martina or Peter was probably a better fit for the job; I just happened to fill it instead. I have definitely learned that it is the team leader’s job to get people moving and that indecisive leadership wastes time and good effort from other teammates. I think our team did well with the limited time we had to get this project done and, individually, members like Peter and William went above and beyond to get the work done fast. However, as a group, we suffered from poor communication, time management, and bad leadership.
